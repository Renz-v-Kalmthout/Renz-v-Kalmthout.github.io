My motivation:

Theory:

Definition:

Textbook definition according to the Cambridge Dictionary:

"The willingness to do something, or something that causes this willingness."

  • Intinsic motivation
  • Extrinsic motivation

    • However looking at more examples shows that "http://changingminds.org/explanations/motivation/four_motivations.htm" Gives us two more variants on motivations that are also very applicable to current day:

  • Introjected motivation
  • Identified motivation

    • Intrinsic motivation:

    This type of motivation is mostly based on internal wants and needs. E.g.: "I am hungry, I am going to eat something." But it is not limited to being curious, or needed to be loved.

    Extrinsic motivation:

    This type of motivation is based on external wants and needs. For example: "Studying harder for a test, to get a better grade." This does not mean it doesn't have an effect on the internal feeling of an individual.

    Introjected motivation:

    It feels simmular to Intrinsic motivation, however if not done the person will feel guilty.

    Identified motivation:

    This means that the person knows he needs to do something, but hasn't done anything about it yet.

    Preformance motivation:

    Je bent in redelijke mate intrinsiek gemotiveerd en maar weinig extrinsiek gemotiveerd. Dat je redelijk intrinsiek gemotiveerd bent betekent dat je het best leuk vindt om te studeren. Maar héél leuk vind je het nou ook weer niet. Dat je weinig extrinsiek gemotiveerd bent wil zeggen dat het je weinig kan schelen wat je met je studie kunt bereiken: een diploma, geld, status of de erkenning van de mensen waar je van houdt. Je interesses zijn voor jou belangrijker.
